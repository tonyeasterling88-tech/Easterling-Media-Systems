Systems Note: Let the Archive Do Its Job

Published: 2026-06-29 · Author: Tony Easterling

An archive should make the work easier to understand, not harder. This week I have been returning to a simple standard: the public record should say what is live, what is still an experiment, and what has moved on.

The practical work

That means keeping the Builds page, blog, newsletter archive, and project pages connected to the same reality. It also means using the archive to show progress without dressing every unfinished idea up as a product.

The June newsletter carries the same thread: reliable publishing, a cleaner record of what shipped, early tester intake, and careful MVP sequencing. Those pieces reinforce each other when they are easy to find in one place.

What I am avoiding

An archive can become a trophy shelf full of stale promises. I would rather show fewer things clearly than keep a long list that needs explanation. Retiring, relabeling, or removing an old path is part of maintaining trust.

Lesson learned

Visibility creates leverage only when it is accurate. A useful archive is a decision tool for both the builder and the person trying to understand the work.
